Slime

  • In your .emacs file, point your Lisp implementation to the eql executable, adding the command line option -slime: 
      (setq inferior-lisp-program "/path-to-eql/eql -slime")
  • For help in Slime mode, see the qapropos and qgui functions.
  • Qt event processing is near-to-native, see eql/slime/process-qt-events.lisp.

Known problem (experienced in OSX only)

Since there may (at least on some OS) happen situations where Emacs/Slime freeze (experienced randomly after hitting the Tab key), you'll find an "Emergency Hook" window (minimized on startup).

So, if freezing happens, bring back the minimized window, and take some action, e.g. click on the (break) button: this will cause Emacs/Slime to respond again.
